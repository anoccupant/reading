Planck and Bohm the Men Whose Fate Is “Uncomputed”
――Part 6: The Ultimate Reality――

Characters:
• The Objective Observer (Salviati): One who possesses deep insights into Digital Signal Theory and the discrete universe.
• The Existing Intellect (Simplicio): One who reveres Quantum Mechanics as a "sublime truth."
• The Seeker (Sagredo): One who seeks to unravel the universe's specifications through simple logic.


Act I: The Formidable Complex Number
Sagredo: By the way, we spoke earlier of complex numbers—how the phase information they allow us to express is the very foundation of quantum mechanics. But that isn't the only information a complex number holds. A complex number, by its very nature, possesses information regarding its "direction of rotation." I have realized that this information is what divides reality itself.

Act II: Our Universe
Simplicio: Direction of rotation? What could that possibly mean in a physical sense?
Sagredo: In our world, the curl operator signifies a right-handed rotation. This can be rephrased as our world having the property of being represented by complex basis functions with a right-handed rotational direction—that is, positive frequency components.

Act III: The Alias of Our Universe
Simplicio: Then what of a world represented by complex basis functions with negative frequency components?
Sagredo: That would be a world requiring a left-handed curl operator. There, time would flow backward, and electrons would be subject to an opposite Lorentz force. What exists there is antimatter. The true nature of what physicists feared as "time flowing backward" is simply this negative rotational component.
Simplicio: ……
Salviati: Correct. Our physical laws adopt the "right-hand rule" simply because our universe selected right-handed rotation as its fundamental operation. Since mathematics permits both right-handed and left-handed rotation, the Architect’s computing engine outputs both as "reality."

Act IV: The Ultimate Truth
Sagredo: The ultimate truth revealed in this New Dialogue is the universal specification that "whatever exists mathematically, exists in reality." One might even call it the specification of the Architect's computing engine.
Sagredo: Precisely. The entire stage was a Four-Dimensional Digital Matrix Memory Space where computation and self-recording are repeated. And "computation" was the transition of phase information—it was "time" itself. The universe was not playing dice. What we perceived as "probability" was merely the "quantization error" that inevitably occurs when outputting computational results onto a digital matrix with a lattice size of $h$.

(Curtain Falls)
