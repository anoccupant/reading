Planck and Bohm the Men Whose Fate Is “Uncomputed”
――Part 5: The Inertia-Less Medium――

Characters:
• The Objective Observer (Salviati): One who possesses deep insights into Digital Signal Theory and the discrete universe.
• The Existing Intellect (Simplicio): One who reveres Quantum Mechanics as a "sublime truth."
• The Seeker (Sagredo): One who seeks to unravel the universe's specifications through simple logic.


Act I: The Towering Wall of Light Speed Once Again
Simplicio: But no matter what reality exists, the theory of relativity does not permit the propagation of information to exceed the speed of light. Your so-called "Universal System Clock" is a non-scientific leap.

Act II: The Inertia-Less Medium
Salviati: Let us conduct a thought experiment. Propagation, as we defined it, is a relationship of forcing one's own displacement upon a neighboring medium, thereby receiving a reaction. But what if we assume a medium that possesses no inertia (mass)?
Sagredo: In a resistance-free medium without inertia, the displacement would propagate instantly, traveling at an infinite speed.
Simplicio: Absurd. How could such a signal carry energy?
Salviati: No. It is because it carries no energy that its speed is infinite.
Simplicio: ……

Act III: The True Nature of the System Clock
Sagredo: I see. That’s it. No inertia means no reaction. No inertia means no time is required for displacement; because no time is required, there is no constraint on propagation speed. Since it carries no information, it carries no energy; and for that which carries no energy, the constraint of the speed of light does not apply.
Salviati: Exactly. By simply establishing the universal specification of the universe as an "inertia-less medium," an infinite-speed system clock is realized without contradiction, even while remaining within the conventional concept of "propagation."

Act IV: The Creator of the Universe
Simplicio: Nonsense... How can something that doesn't even possess energy be of any use? Surely something is wrong. God would never create such a universe.

(Curtain Falls)
